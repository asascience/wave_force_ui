Description:

The National Oceanographic Data Center NODC maintains a database that holds wind, wave, and other marine data collected by the NOAA National Data Buoy Center (NDBC). The data are collected from NDBC moored buoys and from C-MAN (Coastal-Marine Automated Network) stations located on piers, offshore towers, lighthouses, and beaches. Parameters reported by both buoys and C-MAN stations include air temperature and pressure, wind speed and direction, wind gust, and sea surface temperature. The buoys (and a few C-MAN stations located on offshore towers) also report wave data, usually including wave height, wave period, and wave spectra. Since the late 1980s some buoys have reported directional wave spectra. NODC receives the data from NDBC on a monthly basis, generally 2-3 weeks after the last observation from the given month, and makes them available online.

 

As part of a data modernization effort, the National Data Buoy Center (NDBC) has updated the archival data file format of their Coastal-Marine Automated Network (C-MAN) stations and moored (weather) buoys to netCDF. This update facilitates inclusion of additional parameters, as well as improvements to the metadata. On 26 May 2011, NODC's NOAA Marine Environmental Buoy Database began disseminating NDBC’s archival data in these netCDF files, retroactively to the monthly January 2011 data. Data are available in both F291 and netCDF formats for January through March 2011. Data prior to January 2011 will remain in the F291 format.

 

XWaves can import NODC data files two ways: from a file saved to local disk (Disk File) or downloaded right from the NODC FTP site (WWW Download). The download option is only available for the F291 data format prior to January 2011. Both types, can be downloaded for free from the NODC website.

 

• NetCDF File naming convention: NDBC_<WMO ID>_YYYYMM_D<DEPLOYMENT NUMBER>_v<VERSION NUMBER>.nc

• WMO ID: 5-character alpha-numeric string, which the World Meteorological Organization assigned, to identify C-MAN stations and moored buoys;

• YYYYMM: numeric year and month that NDBC collected the data;

• DEPLOYMENT NUMBER: unique number assigned to specific station configuration, sequentially assigned by the NDBC database system

(lower number = older configuration and a higher number = a newer configuration);

• VERSION NUMBER: two-digit number that NDBC uses to track their version of the data, starting with 00;

• .nc: netCDF (version 4) file extension;

Format:

NetCDF Variables

time time (seconds since 1970-01-01 00:00:00 UTC (UNIX))

nominal_latitude Decimal latitude *Optional

nominal_longitude Decimal longitude *Optional

wave_wpm frequency vector (sea_surface_wave_frequency (Hz))

c11 sea surface wave variance spectral density (m^2/Hz)

wind_speed Wind speed (m/s) *Optional

wind_speed_qc Flags for good quality wind data *Optional

wind_direction Wind from direction (degrees clockwise from North) *Optional

wind_direction_qc Flags for good quality wind data *Optional

height_of_instrument Height of anemometer *Optional

 

If 2D Wave Spectra

alpha1 Mean wave direction at specified frequency

alpha2 Principal wave direction at specified frequency

r1 First normalized polar coordinate of the Fourier_coefficients

r2 Last normalized polar coordinate of the Fourier_coefficients

NOTE: DIRECTIONAL WAVE SPECTRUM = C11(f)*D(f,A), f=frequency (Hz), A=Azimuth angle measured clockwise from North to the direction wave is from. D(f,A)=(1/PI)*(0.5+R1*COS(A-ALPHA1)+R2*COS(2*(A-ALPHA2))), in which R1 and R2 are dimensionless and ALPHA1 and ALPHA2 are respectively mean and principal wave directions. In terms of Longuet-Higgins Fourier Coefficients R1=(SQRT(a1*a1+b1*b1))/a0, R2=SQRT(a2*a2+b2*b2))/a0, ALPHA1=270.0-ARCTAN(b1,a1), ALPHA2=270.0-(0.5*ARCTAN(b2,a2)+{0. or 180.}), C11(f) is the nondirectional wave spectra data from RECORD C or RECORD K.
